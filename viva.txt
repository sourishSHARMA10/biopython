Viva Questions with Answers from the PDF
1. What is Biopython?
Answer

Biopython is a Python library used in bioinformatics for working with biological data such as DNA, RNA, and protein sequences.

2. What is a cell?
Answer

A cell is the basic structural and functional unit of life.

3. What is the function of ribosomes?
Answer

Ribosomes produce proteins from amino acids during the process called protein synthesis or translation.

4. What is protein synthesis?
Answer

Protein synthesis is the process in which proteins are produced from amino acids inside living cells.

5. What is DNA?
Answer

DNA is the genetic material present inside the nucleus of the cell that contains instructions for making proteins.

6. What is a gene?
Answer

A gene is a segment of DNA.

7. What are chromosomes?
Answer

Chromosomes are structures formed from tightly packed chromatin and DNA.

8. What are histones?
Answer

Histones are proteins around which DNA strands are wrapped.

9. What are the nitrogen bases in DNA?
Answer

The nitrogen bases in DNA are:

Adenine
Thymine
Guanine
Cytosine
10. What is RNA?
Answer

RNA is a nucleic acid involved in carrying instructions and helping in protein synthesis.

11. What are the types of RNA?
Answer

The three types of RNA are:

mRNA
tRNA
rRNA
12. What is the function of mRNA?
Answer

mRNA carries information from genes for the production of polypeptides in the cytoplasm.

13. What is the function of tRNA?
Answer

tRNA transports amino acids to ribosomes for protein synthesis.

14. What is the function of rRNA?
Answer

rRNA combines with ribosomal proteins to form ribosomes.

15. What is the difference between DNA and RNA?
Answer

DNA is long, double stranded, contains deoxyribose sugar, and uses thymine.
RNA is short, single stranded, contains ribose sugar, and uses uracil.

16. What are macronutrients?
Answer

Macronutrients are nutrients required in large amounts. Examples are:

Carbohydrates
Proteins
Fats
Water
Fiber
17. What are micronutrients?
Answer

Micronutrients are nutrients required in small amounts such as vitamins and minerals.

18. What are proteins?
Answer

Proteins are long chains of amino acids linked together by peptide bonds.

19. What are amino acids?
Answer

Amino acids are organic molecules that serve as the building blocks of proteins.

20. What are the components of an amino acid?
Answer

Each amino acid consists of:

A central carbon
An amino group
A carboxyl group
A hydrogen atom
A unique side chain (R-group)
21. What are essential amino acids?
Answer

Essential amino acids are amino acids that must be obtained from food.

22. What are non-essential amino acids?
Answer

Non-essential amino acids are amino acids that the body can synthesize.

23. Which amino acid plays a crucial role in blood clotting?
Answer

Glutamic Acid (Glu) plays a crucial role in blood clotting.

24. What is fibrinogen?
Answer

Fibrinogen is the protein primarily responsible for blood clotting.

25. What is thrombin?
Answer

Thrombin is the enzyme that converts fibrinogen into fibrin to form a clot.

26. What is transcription?
Answer

Transcription is the process in which a specific gene in DNA is copied into messenger RNA (mRNA).

27. Where does transcription occur?
Answer

Transcription occurs inside the cell nucleus.

28. What is translation?
Answer

Translation is the process in which ribosomes read the mRNA sequence and assemble amino acids into a polypeptide chain.

29. What is the role of ribosomes in translation?
Answer

Ribosomes read the mRNA sequence and assemble amino acids into a polypeptide chain.

30. What is the role of tRNA in translation?
Answer

tRNA brings the correct amino acids during protein synthesis.
